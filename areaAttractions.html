Tidepooling

Tide charts are available from the Fort Ross Store.

Fishing

When in season, visitors with valid fishing licenses can fish for rock fish, salmon, perch, steelhead and smelt.

Abalone Hunting

The Timber Cove area is world-renowned for Red Abalone. Abalone hunting is seasonal and highly regulated by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife. It requires a license and other rigid requirements.

Surfing

The most popular surf spot is Salmon Creek Beach near Bodega Bay. Be sure to check the surf report for up to date information about conditions and dangers.

Rentals and lessons: Bodega Bay Surf Shack

Bodega Headlands

For those seeking beautiful and challenging hiking trails, head for the Bodega Headlands. They offer majestic views along the coast.

Scuba Diving

Certified scuba divers can explore the coast and the wreck of the S.S. Pomona, a ship that sank over 100 years ago and now sits in forty feet of water just off Fort Ross Cove.

Beaches

A nearby sandy beach is Goat Rock State Beach, near Jenner. Continuing south toward Bodega Bay, there are 16 miles of beautiful state park beaches. Ocean swimming along the Northern California coast is NOT safe; the steep beaches, unpredictable conditions, and rip currents cause drownings every year.

Russian River Swimming

Many Russian River beaches offer on-site facilities as well as swimming access. Monte Rio Beach, east on Highway 116, free public beach Casini’s Campground, Duncans Mills, turn right on Moscow Road Willow Creek Environmental Campground, Duncans Mills, Willow Creek Road,  Johnson’s Beach, Guerneville, east on Highway 116, turn right after the Safeway and look for signs, free public beach

Fort Ross State Historic Park

A California State Historic Park showcasing a historic Russian-era fort compound. Located a few miles south at 19005 Highway 1. Currently they are open every Saturday and Sunday for tours. They also have a bookstore, Visitor’s Center, and gift shop. This is a one of the top attractions in Sonoma County for its historical significance, lovely grounds and ocean access. For specific park hours and updated information, call . For tours and programming, call 707.847.4777 or visit fortross.interp@parks.ca.gov.

Kruse Rhododendron Loop Trail

Located in Kruse Rhododendron State Reserve, adjacent to Salt Point State Park, several miles north of Ocean Hideaway. From Highway 1, turn right onto Kruse Ranch Road and drive ½ mile to the trailhead. The best time to visit is when the blossoms are at their peak between mid-April and mid-June. 707.847.3221  The hiking trail is just over 2 miles round trip with a 200 foot elevation gain.
